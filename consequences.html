CONSEQUENCES

The CONSEQUENCES of World War I were profound and far-reaching, reshaping the global landscape in countless ways. The war led to significant political upheavals, with the fall of empires such as the Ottoman, Austro-Hungarian, German, and Russian. Economically, it devastated many nations, leading to widespread poverty and the reallocation of resources. Socially, the war brought about a shift in societal norms, including the roles of women, who entered the workforce in unprecedented numbers. The immense human cost, with millions of lives lost, left deep scars and a collective trauma that shaped the interwar period and laid the groundwork for future conflicts.
