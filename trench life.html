Average Day in the Trenches

MEDICINE

Medicine during WW1 was not as developed as today and mental illnesses were taken as a sign of weakness. If you were ill or injured then you would be categorised into 1 of the 4 categories of the TRIAGE.

TRIAGE

The triage was a way of deciding your next actions after you've had an injury. You next actions are based on your seriousness of your injury.

LEVEL 1

TRIVIAL - Soldiers with minor injuries were treated quickly and returned to the front line.

SERIOUS - These included wounds that necessitated advanced medical care but were not immediately life-threatening

CRITICAL - This stage is for soldiers that needed medical attention ASAP. They were rushed to the neaarest field or proper hospital, but this would only apple if they had a chance of survival.

BEYOND HELP - This is the last stage where soldiers are critical, but they have around 5%-no chance of survival. If this is the case then these soldiers are given the most comfort possible and they write one last letter to any relatives.

AVERAGE DAY IN THE TRENCHES

5AM - Stand to

5.30AM - Rum ration

6AM - Stand-down

7AM - Breakfast

8AM - Cleaning + tidying

NOON - Dinner

NOON to 5PM - Sleep/downtime

5PM - Tea

6PM - Stand to

6.30PM - Stand down

After that - Work all night

DEFINITIONS OF WORDS

STAND TO - guarding the front line of their trenches

STAND DOWN - to go off duty or relax from a state of readiness

WORK - patrol, digging, putting up barbed wire and fighting

SLEEPING CONDITIONS

In the trenches, sleeping took place in the afternoon so it was pretty light outside. This is because most attacks and fighting happened at night

Most people would just lay on the floor of the trench - not exactly you king size bed with maybe a little teddybear. instead of a bed you got the floor and instead of a teddybear you have a gun or ur mates.

If you were luckcy then you get to use a little fire step as a pillow.

DISEASES

>

1) TRENCH FOOT - a condition caused by prolonged exposure to damp, unsanitary, and cold conditions.

2) SHELL SHOCK - a mental illness used to describe psychological trauma experienced by trauma from the war.

3) TYPHUS - Typhus refers to a group of infectious diseases caused by bacterial infection, spread by parasites such as lice, fleas, and mites

SYMPTOMS

1) TRENCH FOOT - itching, or numbness in the feet, The feet may start to swell, Your feet may turn red or bluish due to poor blood circulation.

2) SHELL SHOCK - tremors, headaches, stuttering, confusion

3) TYPHUS - Fever, backache, dull red rash, nausea
