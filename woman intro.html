JOBS OF WOMAN IN WW1

A) AGRICULTURE - They worked on farms, producing food to support the war effort. Organizations like the Women's Land Army were established to recruit women for agricultural work

B) MEDICINE - Women served as nurses, doctors, and support staff in various capacities. They worked in military hospitals, field hospitals, and casualty clearing stations, providing essential medical care to wounded soldiers

C) ENGINEERS - hey worked in munitions factories, assembling weapons and explosives. They also took on roles in transportation, such as driving buses and trains, and in new disciplines like aeronautical and automobile engineering

D) POLICE - : Established by Nina Boyle and Margaret Damer Dawson, the WPS focused on issues affecting women and children. Members of the WPS were volunteers who undertook duties similar to those of male police officers.

E) WEAPONARY - Women were heavily involved in munitions production, working in factories to produce ammunition and other war materials. They handled explosives, operated machinery, and performed various tasks essential to the war effort.

Working in munitions factories during World War I was particularly hazardous for women. They handled dangerous explosives like TNT, which caused severe health issues, turning their skin yellow and earning them the nickname "Canary Girls." Long hours, risky machinery, and the ever-present threat of explosions made these jobs perilous, yet many women undertook them out of a strong sense of duty.
